Bio

I am a Computer Vision Engineer at NODAR focused on untethered 3D perception and calibration for autonomy, using principles of signal processing and deep learning. I completed my Master of Engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) where I concentrated in Artificial Intelligence while studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS). I was advised by Prof. Daniela Rus of the Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL) where I researched computer vision, robotics, and machine learning. I also completed my Bachelor of Science at MIT in EECS and Mathematics, where I spent time working in CSAIL, the MIT Media Lab, and outside of academia at places such as NASA and Draper. Additionally, I completed a minor in Theater Arts.

My research focus has primarily been on deep learning and computer vision, with excursions into reinforcement learning. The impact areas I find most interesting are autonomous driving and planetary science. I served as a team lead for MIT Driverless and TA for MIT's graduate-level applied machine learning course (6.862) and undergraduate machine learning course (6.036). I was also involved in the organization of the MIT EnergyHack.
