Summary

I am an undergrad at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology graduating in Spring 2020. I will receive a B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science and a B.S. in Math, and I'll be pursuing a MEng in the former starting Fall 2020 at MIT. My academic focus has been machine learning, particularly the field of computer vision. The impact areas I find most interesting are agriculture, space exploration, and public policy.

Around campus I have been involved with the EnergyHack as a director, with a fraternity as the treasurer, and briefly as an athlete on the varsity football team. Formerly, I was a tutor for several years through my high school's tutoring center where I tutored students in all levels of math. While my academic focus is primarily in the sciences, I also enjoy music and theater. Thus, I am also pursuing a minor in Theater Arts.

My recent research in the MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory has focused on new neural network layer types for computer vision. Earlier on in the lab, I also researched human decision making for use in artificial agents. Additionally, in the MIT Media Lab, I researched better vision methods for autonomous vehicles in inclement weather conditions. This upcoming summer, I will be researching Competency-Aware Machine Learning at Draper Laboratory in Cambridge.

Professionally, I've held roles ranging from software developer to machine learning engineer at small startups to large government agencies. For the highlights, see my resume below!
